Dr. Sharma is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at the FAMU-FSU College of Engineering. With a background spanning civil engineering and statistics, he leads interdisciplinary research that incorporates economics, data science, governance, insurance, and emergency response to bring a holistic approach to solving complex challenges in disaster resilience.

Before joining FSU, he completed postdoctoral work at the Stanford Urban Resilience Initiative, graduate studies at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, and earned his undergraduate degree from NIT Trichy, India.

A native of Punjab, India, Dr. Sharma's lived experiences with natural hazards and financial hardship deeply inspire his research directions and teaching philosophy. His impact metric is human well-being — from colleagues and students to communities worldwide. Outside research, he enjoys DIY projects, poetry, cooking, and chess.

What We Do

Research Areas

Infrastructure Resilience
Modeling and analysis of interconnected infrastructure systems under natural hazards and extreme events, optimizing recovery strategies and system performance.
Disaster Risk Modeling
Quantitative probabilistic methods for assessing disaster risk, uncertainty quantification, and multi-hazard risk frameworks for regional infrastructure systems.
Transportation Systems
Smart transportation, pedestrian safety, evacuation modeling, equitable mobility, and AI-driven analysis of transportation infrastructure and networks.
Hydrology & Climate Risk
Flood hazard modeling, watershed-scale hydrologic assessments, climate variability impacts on water systems, and resilient water infrastructure design.
AI, ML & GIS
Geospatial intelligence, digital twin development, Web-GIS platforms, machine learning for disaster prediction, and spatial analytics for infrastructure risk mapping.
Structural Engineering
Structural performance assessment, infrastructure reliability under extreme loads, resilience of reinforced concrete systems, and lifecycle engineering analysis.
