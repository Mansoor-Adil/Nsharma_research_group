Dr. Neetesh Sharma

Dr. Neetesh Sharma

Department of Civil & Environmental Engineering

🌐 Personal Website 🎓 Google Scholar 🏢 RIDER Profile

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at the FAMU-FSU College of Engineering. With a background spanning civil engineering and statistics, I lead interdisciplinary research integrating economics, data science, governance, insurance, and emergency response to develop holistic solutions for disaster resilience.

Before joining FSU, I completed postdoctoral work at the Stanford Urban Resilience Initiative, graduate studies at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, and earned my undergraduate degree from NIT Trichy, India.

As a native of Punjab, India, my lived experience with natural hazards and access to foundational resources deeply shapes my research vision. My impact metric is human well-being — from students and colleagues to communities worldwide. Outside of research, I enjoy DIY projects, poetry, cooking, and chess.

Workshop Overview
Wildfire dynamics are increasingly shaped by compound drivers, including climate variability, fuel dynamics, land-use change, and rapid expansion of the Wildland–Urban Interface. Advances in artificial intelligence and large-scale spatio-temporal data are creating new opportunities to improve wildfire prediction, operational infrastructure, and decision support systems.
